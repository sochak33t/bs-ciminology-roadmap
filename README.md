# bs-ciminology-roadmap

# BS Criminology Complete Roadmap (Philippines, CHED CMO-Aligned)

> Based on CHED Memorandum Order No. 5, s. 2018 (Policies, Standards and Guidelines for BS Criminology), and the Competency-Based Curriculum used by most Philippine criminology schools (e.g., PLM, PUP, NCBA, Lyceum, San Beda). Actual subject titles/units vary slightly per school (unit range: 158–165 units total), but content coverage is standard because all schools must prepare students for the same PRC Criminologist Licensure Examination (CLE).

---

## PROGRAM OVERVIEW

| Component | Approx. Units |
|---|---|
| General Education (GE) | 36–39 |
| PE | 8 (4 courses) |
| NSTP | 6 (2 courses, non-credit for grading but required) |
| Criminology Professional Courses | 100–110 |
| Practicum/OJT | 3–6 |
| Research (Thesis 1 & 2) | 6 |
| **Total** | **~160 units, 4 years** |

Core professional clusters tested in the board exam:
1. **Criminal Jurisprudence and Procedure** (Criminal Law + Procedure + Evidence + Human Rights)
2. **Law Enforcement Administration** (Police Organization, Industrial Security, Personnel Mgt.)
3. **Criminalistics** (Forensic Science — Ballistics, QD, Fingerprinting, Polygraphy, Legal Medicine)
4. **Crime Detection and Investigation** (Investigation, Intelligence, Personal ID)
5. **Correctional Administration** (Corrections, Penology, Community-Based Corrections)
6. **Criminology / Criminal Sociology** (Theories of Crime Causation, Juvenile Delinquency, Ethics, Logic)

# YEAR 1

## 1st Semester

### General Education: Understanding the Self
- **Objectives:** Understand the biological, psychological, and social dimensions of the self.
- **Topics:** Self from philosophical, sociological, anthropological, psychological perspectives; digital self.
- **Practical Exercises:** Self-reflection journal, values clarification exercise.
- **Resources:** *Understanding the Self* (Sanchez, Rex Bookstore); CHED GE Curriculum guide.

### General Education: Readings in Philippine History
- **Objectives:** Analyze Philippine history using primary sources.
- **Topics:** Pre-colonial society, Spanish colonization, revolution, American/Japanese periods, contemporary issues.
- **Practical Exercises:** Primary source critique (e.g., Doctrina Christiana, Noli Me Tangere excerpts).
- **Resources:** Zaide, *Philippine History*; Agoncillo, *History of the Filipino People*.

### General Education: Mathematics in the Modern World
- **Objectives:** Apply mathematical reasoning to real-world/statistical problems.
- **Topics:** Patterns, sets, statistics basics, logic, geometry in nature.
- **Practical Exercises:** Data-set analysis, basic statistics computation.
- **Resources:** Aufmann, *Mathematics in the Modern World*.

### Introduction to Criminology
- **Objectives:** Define criminology as a science; distinguish it from criminal justice, penology, victimology.
- **Topics:** History and definition of criminology; branches (criminal anthropology, criminal sociology, criminal psychology/psychiatry, penology, criminalistics, victimology); careers in criminology.
- **Practical Exercises:** Career-mapping paper; interview a licensed criminologist.
- **Resources:** Soriano, *Criminology* (Wiseman's Books); Sison, *Fundamentals of Criminology*.

### Philippine Constitution
- **Objectives:** Understand constitutional principles governing the state, citizenship, and rights.
- **Topics:** 1987 Constitution — Bill of Rights, citizenship, suffrage, government structure.
- **Practical Exercises:** Case digest of a Bill of Rights Supreme Court case.
- **Resources:** Cruz, *Philippine Political Law*; 1987 Constitution full text.

### NSTP 1 (CWTS or ROTC)
- **Objectives:** Instill civic consciousness and community service orientation (or military training for ROTC).
- **Topics:** Community immersion, disaster preparedness, citizenship training.
- **Practical Exercises:** Community service project.
- **Resources:** CHED NSTP Manual.

### PE 1 — Movement Enhancement/Physical Fitness
- **Objectives:** Assess and improve personal physical fitness.
- **Topics:** Physical fitness testing, exercise physiology basics.
- **Practical Exercises:** Fitness test battery, personal fitness plan.
- **Resources:** DepEd/CHED PE Curriculum Guide.

---

## 2nd Semester

### General Education: Art Appreciation
- **Objectives:** Appreciate art forms and their social functions.
- **Topics:** Elements of art, Philippine art forms, art criticism.
- **Practical Exercises:** Museum/gallery critique paper.

### General Education: Science, Technology and Society
- **Objectives:** Evaluate the interaction of science, technology, and society.
- **Topics:** Scientific method, biotech, ICT, environment, RA on cybercrime/data privacy.
- **Practical Exercises:** STS issue position paper (e.g., surveillance tech and privacy).

### Criminal Law 1 (Revised Penal Code — Book 1)
- **Objectives:** Master general principles of criminal liability under the RPC.
- **Topics:** Definition of felonies; stages of execution; conspiracy; circumstances affecting criminal liability (justifying, exempting, mitigating, aggravating, alternative); persons criminally liable; penalties; extinction of criminal liability.
- **Practical Exercises:** Case digests (People v. Oanis, People v. Genosa); penalty computation drills.
- **Resources:** Reyes, *The Revised Penal Code Book One*; Boado, *Notes and Cases on the RPC*.

### Philippine History and Government (if separate from GE)
- **Objectives:** Understand structures of Philippine government relevant to law enforcement.
- **Topics:** Executive, legislative, judicial branches; local government units; PNP/BJMP/BuCor placement in government structure.
- **Practical Exercises:** Organizational chart mapping exercise.

### Criminological Research 1 (Methods of Research)
- **Objectives:** Understand basic research concepts applicable to criminology.
- **Topics:** Research process, quantitative vs. qualitative methods, ethics in research.
- **Practical Exercises:** Draft a research problem statement.
- **Resources:** Calmorin, *Research Methods and Thesis Writing*.

### NSTP 2
- **Objectives:** Continue civic training component.
- **Topics:** Leadership training, values formation, service project completion.

### PE 2 — Rhythmic Activities / Individual & Dual Sports
- **Objectives:** Develop motor skills through sports.
- **Topics:** Basic sports skills, rules, rhythm activities.

# YEAR 2

## 1st Semester

### Criminal Law 2 (Revised Penal Code — Book 2)
- **Objectives:** Identify elements of specific crimes against persons, property, chastity, etc.
- **Topics:** Crimes against national security, public order, public interest, persons (murder, homicide, physical injuries), property (theft, robbery, estafa), chastity, civil status, honor.
- **Practical Exercises:** Element-matching drills; mock case classification.
- **Resources:** Reyes, *RPC Book Two*; Regalado, *Criminal Law Conspectus*.

### Criminal Procedure and Evidence 1
- **Objectives:** Understand rules governing criminal prosecution.
- **Topics:** Rules of Court (Rules 110–127); jurisdiction; arrest, search and seizure; bail; arraignment; trial.
- **Practical Exercises:** Draft a criminal complaint/information.
- **Resources:** Rules of Court (Criminal Procedure); Riano, *Criminal Procedure*.

### Introduction to Law Enforcement Administration
- **Objectives:** Understand the organization of Philippine law enforcement agencies.
- **Topics:** PNP history and mandate (RA 6975, RA 8551), organizational structure, ranks.
- **Practical Exercises:** PNP organizational chart reconstruction.
- **Resources:** RA 6975, RA 8551, RA 8551-related IRR; PNP Manuals.

### Philosophy of Man / Logic and Critical Thinking
- **Objectives:** Apply logical reasoning to legal/criminological arguments.
- **Topics:** Deductive/inductive reasoning, fallacies, syllogisms.
- **Practical Exercises:** Fallacy-spotting exercise using court decisions.

### Criminological Research 2 (Statistics)
- **Objectives:** Apply statistical tools to crime data.
- **Topics:** Descriptive and inferential statistics, crime rate computation, SPSS basics.
- **Practical Exercises:** Analyze PNP crime statistics dataset.
- **Resources:** Calmorin, *Statistics in Education and the Sciences*.

## 2nd Semester

### Criminal Procedure and Evidence 2 (Law on Evidence)
- **Objectives:** Understand admissibility and weight of evidence.
- **Topics:** Rules 128–134 of Rules of Court; burden of proof; hearsay; documentary/object/testimonial evidence; chain of custody.
- **Practical Exercises:** Mock direct/cross-examination.
- **Resources:** Riano, *Evidence*; Regalado, *Remedial Law Compendium Vol. 2*.

### Criminal Investigation 1
- **Objectives:** Learn fundamental principles of criminal investigation.
- **Topics:** Investigation process, crime scene management, interview vs. interrogation, case build-up.
- **Practical Exercises:** Mock crime scene sketch and report writing.
- **Resources:** Soriano, *Criminal Investigation*; Rodell Cruz, *Criminal Investigation and Detection*.

### Personal Identification Techniques (Dactyloscopy)
- **Objectives:** Master fingerprint classification systems.
- **Topics:** Henry classification, ridge patterns, AFIS, other ID methods (Bertillon system).
- **Practical Exercises:** Fingerprint classification worksheet, ink-rolling exercise.
- **Resources:** Ronquillo, *Personal Identification Techniques*.

### Criminal Sociology
- **Objectives:** Analyze crime as a social phenomenon.
- **Topics:** Theories of crime causation (classical, positivist, sociological, biological, psychological); social disorganization; strain theory; differential association.
- **Practical Exercises:** Theory-application case study.
- **Resources:** Siegel, *Criminology: Theories, Patterns, and Typologies*.

### Human Behavior and Victimology
- **Objectives:** Understand victim-offender dynamics.
- **Topics:** Victimology theories, victim typologies, RA 8505 (Rape Victim Assistance), victim compensation.
- **Practical Exercises:** Victim-impact case analysis.

### PE 4 — Aquatics/Recreational Activities

# YEAR 3

## 1st Semester

### Criminal Law 3 (Special Penal Laws)
- **Objectives:** Apply special laws frequently used in practice.
- **Topics:** RA 9165 (Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act), RA 9262 (VAWC), RA 7610 (Child Abuse), RA 3019 (Anti-Graft), RA 10591 (Firearms), Cybercrime Prevention Act (RA 10175), RA 9208 (Anti-Trafficking).
- **Practical Exercises:** Special law case digesting; elements-matrix construction.
- **Resources:** Boado, *Notes on Special Penal Laws*.

### Criminalistics 1 (Forensic Ballistics)
- **Objectives:** Understand firearms identification and examination.
- **Topics:** Firearm mechanism, bullet/cartridge identification, gunshot residue, trajectory analysis.
- **Practical Exercises:** Cartridge case comparison lab (simulated), ballistic report writing.
- **Resources:** Fajardo, *Forensic Ballistics*.

### Law Enforcement Administration 1 (Police Organization and Administration)
- **Objectives:** Understand police management principles.
- **Topics:** Police organizational structure, span of control, unity of command, POP (Police Operational Procedures).
- **Practical Exercises:** Police station organizational audit exercise.
- **Resources:** PNP Operations Manual (latest edition).

### Correctional Administration 1 (Institutional Corrections/Penology)
- **Objectives:** Understand the Philippine penal/correctional system.
- **Topics:** History of penology, BuCor mandate, prison classification, RA 10575 (Bureau of Corrections Act).
- **Practical Exercises:** Prison-system comparative table (Philippines vs. other countries).
- **Resources:** Bernardo, *Introduction to Corrections*.

## 2nd Semester

### Criminal Law 4 (Trial and Moot Court / Criminal Law Review)
- **Objectives:** Synthesize criminal law and procedure through simulation.
- **Topics:** Full case simulation from filing to judgment.
- **Practical Exercises:** Moot court exercise (prosecution vs. defense roles).

### Criminalistics 2 (Questioned Documents Examination)
- **Objectives:** Detect forgery and analyze handwriting.
- **Topics:** Handwriting analysis, ink/paper examination, forgery detection.
- **Practical Exercises:** Signature comparison exercise.
- **Resources:** Osborn, *Questioned Documents* (classic reference).

### Criminalistics 3 (Forensic Photography)
- **Objectives:** Apply photography techniques to crime scene documentation.
- **Topics:** Camera principles, crime scene photography protocols, digital forensic imaging.
- **Practical Exercises:** Mock crime-scene photo documentation set.

### Law Enforcement Administration 2 (Industrial Security Management)
- **Objectives:** Understand private security concepts.
- **Topics:** RA 5487 (Private Security Agency Law), physical security, personnel security, VIP security.
- **Practical Exercises:** Security survey/plan for a sample facility.
- **Resources:** Gaddi, *Industrial Security Management*.

### Correctional Administration 2 (Community-Based Corrections/Parole and Probation)
- **Objectives:** Understand non-institutional corrections.
- **Topics:** Probation Law (PD 968), parole, pardon, RA 10707; Katarungang Pambarangay.
- **Practical Exercises:** Probation case study analysis.

### Criminological Research 3 (Thesis Writing 1)
- **Objectives:** Develop a research proposal.
- **Topics:** Chapters 1–3 of thesis (Introduction, Review of Related Literature, Methodology).
- **Practical Exercises:** Thesis proposal draft and proposal defense.

# YEAR 4

## 1st Semester

### Criminalistics 4 (Legal Medicine)
- **Objectives:** Apply medico-legal principles to criminal investigation.
- **Topics:** Wounds, cause/manner/time of death, forensic pathology basics, sexual crimes medico-legal exam.
- **Practical Exercises:** Medico-legal certificate analysis.
- **Resources:** Solis, *Legal Medicine*.

### Criminalistics 5 (Polygraphy/Forensic Psychology)
- **Objectives:** Understand deception-detection tools and forensic psychological assessment.
- **Topics:** Polygraph theory and limitations, forensic psychological profiling.
- **Practical Exercises:** Case-based profiling exercise.

### Crime Detection and Investigation (Special Crime Investigation)
- **Objectives:** Investigate specific crime types (arson, kidnapping, bombing, sex crimes, robbery).
- **Topics:** Modus operandi analysis, specialized investigation techniques per crime type.
- **Practical Exercises:** Special-crime investigation case simulation.

### Criminal Justice System and Human Rights Education
- **Objectives:** Analyze the Philippine CJS pillars and human rights framework.
- **Topics:** Five pillars of CJS (community, law enforcement, prosecution, courts, corrections); RA 9745 (Anti-Torture); international human rights instruments.
- **Practical Exercises:** CJS-pillar coordination case study.

### Criminological Research 4 (Thesis Writing 2)
- **Objectives:** Complete and defend the research study.
- **Topics:** Data gathering, analysis (Chapter 4), conclusions/recommendations (Chapter 5), final defense.
- **Practical Exercises:** Full thesis manuscript, oral defense.

### Practicum/OJT Orientation Seminar
- **Objectives:** Prepare students for field deployment.
- **Topics:** OJT requirements, agency coordination (PNP, BJMP, BuCor, NBI, private security).

---

## 2nd Semester

### Field Practicum/On-the-Job Training (OJT)
- **Objectives:** Apply classroom knowledge in actual law enforcement/correctional/security settings.
- **Topics/Deployment sites:** PNP stations, BJMP jails, BuCor facilities, NBI, prosecutor's offices, private security agencies, LGU peace and order councils.
- **Practical Exercises:** Daily journal/logbook, agency evaluation, OJT terminal report and presentation.
- **Requirements:** Typically 150–240 hours depending on school; MOA between school and host agency; liability insurance.

### Comprehensive Examination (Pre-Board Review Subject)
- **Objectives:** Consolidate knowledge across all six board exam areas.
- **Topics:** Full-coverage review — Criminal Law/Procedure/Evidence, Law Enforcement Administration, Criminalistics, Crime Detection and Investigation, Correctional Administration, Criminology/Ethics.
- **Practical Exercises:** Mock board exams, subject-area diagnostic tests.
- **Resources:** Review centers (e.g., R.A. Gapuz Review Center, Sison Review Center) and their reviewers.

### Rizal's Life, Works and Writings (GE, mandated by RA 1425)
- **Objectives:** Understand Rizal's role in Philippine nationalism.
- **Topics:** Noli Me Tangere, El Filibusterismo, Rizal's biography, retraction controversy.

### Ethics and Values Education for Criminologists
- **Objectives:** Internalize professional ethical standards.
- **Topics:** Code of Ethics for Criminologists, RA 11131 (Philippine Criminology Profession Act), professional responsibility.

# BOARD EXAM SUBJECT MATRIX (PRC Criminologist Licensure Examination)

Governing law: **RA 11131** (Philippine Criminology Profession Act of 2018), administered by the PRC Board of Criminology.

| Board Subject | Approx. Weight | Key Sub-Topics |
|---|---|---|
| Criminal Jurisprudence, Procedure, and Evidence | ~20% | RPC Books 1 & 2, Special Penal Laws, Rules of Court (110-134), Human Rights laws |
| Law Enforcement Administration | ~20% | Police organization/administration, Industrial security, Police patrol/operations, Traffic management |
| Criminalistics | ~20% | Ballistics, QD examination, Fingerprinting, Forensic photography, Legal medicine, Polygraphy |
| Crime Detection and Investigation | ~15% | Criminal investigation, Interview/interrogation, Special crime investigation, Intelligence |
| Correctional Administration | ~15% | Institutional & non-institutional corrections, Penology, Parole/probation |
| Criminology (Criminal Sociology) | ~10% | Theories of crime, Juvenile delinquency (RA 9344), Ethics, Logic |

*(Exact weighting is set by the PRC Board Resolution for each exam window — candidates should always check the current PRC Program of Examination before final review.)*

# SELF-STUDY SYLLABUS TEMPLATE (Apply to Any Subject Above)

For each subject, structure independent study in 4 stages:

1. **Orientation (Week 1):** Read the subject's IRR/law/textbook introduction; build a topic outline; identify all legal bases (RA/PD/RPC articles).
2. **Core Content (Weeks 2–5):** Study each topic with the "3-pass method" — (a) skim for structure, (b) detailed read with note-taking/outlining, (c) active recall via self-quizzing.
3. **Application (Week 6):** Solve case problems, past board exam questions, and practical exercises listed above.
4. **Mastery Check (Week 7):** Take a 50-item mock quiz; review wrong answers; teach the topic aloud (Feynman technique) to confirm retention.

# COMPETENCY CHECKLIST BEFORE TAKING THE BOARD EXAM

**Criminal Law, Procedure & Evidence**
- [ ] Can state elements of all major RPC crimes from memory
- [ ] Can distinguish justifying/exempting/mitigating/aggravating circumstances
- [ ] Can outline the criminal procedure flow from complaint to judgment
- [ ] Can apply rules on admissibility of evidence and chain of custody
- [ ] Knows key special penal laws (RA 9165, 9262, 7610, 10591, 10175, 3019)

**Law Enforcement Administration**
- [ ] Understands PNP organizational structure and chain of command
- [ ] Can explain police patrol operations and traffic management principles
- [ ] Knows private security licensing requirements (RA 5487)
- [ ] Understands personnel management and police ethical standards

**Criminalistics**
- [ ] Can classify fingerprints using the Henry System
- [ ] Can identify basic firearm/ammunition characteristics
- [ ] Can outline steps in questioned document examination
- [ ] Understands legal medicine terms (cause/manner of death, wound types)
- [ ] Understands polygraph theory and limitations

**Crime Detection and Investigation**
- [ ] Can process a crime scene properly (documentation, chain of custody)
- [ ] Knows interview vs. interrogation techniques
- [ ] Can outline special crime investigation procedures (kidnapping, arson, sex crimes)

**Correctional Administration**
- [ ] Understands institutional vs. non-institutional corrections
- [ ] Knows parole, probation, and pardon distinctions
- [ ] Understands GCTA computation basics (RA 10592)

**Criminology/Criminal Sociology**
- [ ] Can explain major theories of crime causation
- [ ] Understands juvenile justice system (RA 9344)
- [ ] Knows the Code of Ethics for Criminologists

**General Readiness**
- [ ] Consistently scores 75%+ on full-length mock board exams across all six subjects
- [ ] Has completed OJT/practicum hours and terminal report
- [ ] Has completed and defended undergraduate thesis
- [ ] Is updated on recent Supreme Court decisions and PNP/BJMP/BuCor circulars
